# kramers-kronig
Kramers Kronig Relations applied to the index of refraction of Diamond. 

Task
- Obtain tabulated values of the imaginary part of the refractive index, k(λ), for diamond covering
at least the deep-UV through the near-IR. Cite the source you use.
- Numerically evaluate the Kramers–Kronig integral to compute n(ω) from k(ω). Plot n and k
versus wavelength on a common axis.
- Compare your reconstructed n(λ) in the visible (say 400–800 nm) against the tabulated n(λ) from
the same source. Quantify the agreement.
- Now repeat the KK calculation with the UV absorption band (below ~230 nm) artificially set to
zero. Plot the resulting n(λ) in the visible and briefly comment on see. Anything you can
conclude about the physical origin of diamond’s visible high refractive index?

Learning takeaways
- The KK relations seem very fundamental in the domain of signal responses, because they can be applied to any causal signal. This lends credibility to the idea of "complex electric succeptibility" having further physcial meaning than just being a mathematical trick.
